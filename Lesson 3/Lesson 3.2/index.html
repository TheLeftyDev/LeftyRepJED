Goldtop (1952-1958)

The 1952 Gibson Les Paul model is quite literally the gold standard when it comes to Gibson-made solidbody electric guitars. It revolutionized Gibson's position in a booming industry and paved the way for some of the most iconic guitars ever produced. It's no surprise that some of these original examples carry some of the best foundational qualities of what was to come, even in their infancy. This late 1952 Gibson Les Paul Goldtop left the factory in its first-version configuration, which featured a long trapeze under-wraparound tailpiece. A common issue that plagued many of these first-year examples was a shallow neck set from the factory and the use of a bridge that wasn't ergonomically friendly. By the middle of 1953, Gibson had corrected this with a new neck set angle/pitch and the introduction of the wraparound tailpiece, greatly improving playability, tuning, and sustain.
